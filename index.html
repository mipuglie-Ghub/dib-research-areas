Multi-Scale Control of Large Multi-Agent Systems

Large populations of interacting agents (robots, traffic, biological collectives) require control strategies that link individual dynamics to emergent group behavior. We develop multi-scale control architectures—based on continuification and systematic macro–micro bridges—to achieve scalable control with rigorous guarantees.

Analysis and Control of Complex Networks

Network structure strongly shapes collective dynamics such as synchronization, consensus, and coordination across engineered, biological, and social systems. We build theory and methods for sparse, distributed interventions—acting on selected nodes, edges, or adaptive topologies—to steer network behavior under limited actuation and information.

Learn more

Learning and Data-Driven Control of Complex Systems

Many modern control problems involve uncertain, high-dimensional dynamics that cannot be reliably modeled from first principles. We combine control-theoretic guarantees with data-driven learning—adaptive control, reinforcement learning, and control-tutored deep RL—to improve robustness, safety, and sample efficiency.

Analysis and Control of Piecewise-smooth and Hybrid Systems

Many dynamical systems exhibit discontinuous behavior due to impacts, switching, friction, or designed discontinuous control laws. Our group has pioneered the mathematical analysis of such piecewise-smooth (PWS) and hybrid systems, developing comprehensive theories for their bifurcations, stability, and control.

Cybergenetics and Synthetic Biology

Synthetic biology aims to endow living cells with new functionalities through engineered genetic circuits and feedback regulation. We design and experimentally validate cybergenetic control strategies—single-cell and population-level, in vivo and microfluidic—to implement robust closed-loop regulation in microbial systems.

Applications Across Domains

Real-world applications are essential both to demonstrate impact and to reveal new theoretical challenges in complex dynamical systems. We validate our methods on platforms spanning multi-robot systems, biological collectives, human coordination, transportation, and power/infrastructure networks.
