Applications Across Domains

Our theoretical work is grounded in real-world applications and experimental validation, which both demonstrate impact and generate new theoretical challenges.

Application domains

Multi-Robot Systems and Human-Machine Interaction

Our experimental platforms enable validation of shepherding, density control, and distributed coordination algorithms using physical robots and mixed real–virtual environments. These platforms support scalable experiments and direct comparison between analytical and learning-based control strategies.

Biological Systems and Active Matter

We apply multi-scale control concepts to living systems, including microbial populations, synthetic gene networks, and collective animal behavior. These systems naturally exhibit hierarchical organization and indirect control mechanisms, making them ideal testbeds for continuum and field-theoretic control approaches.

Human Coordination and Social Systems

Our work on human groups investigates leadership emergence, synchronization, and collective decision-making, combining experimental studies with control-oriented modeling. These results inform the design of artificial agents and avatars for human–machine interaction.

Transportation and Infrastructure Systems

Applications include vehicle platooning, traffic flow modeling and control, and distributed control of infrastructure systems, where population-level descriptions and robustness are essential.

Power Systems and Infrastructure

Building on our extensive work on power converter dynamics and bifurcations, we have developed distributed control strategies for power grid islanding, transient stability analysis using bifurcation theory, and coordination protocols for multi-agent energy systems. Our work addresses challenges in voltage control, frequency regulation, and intentional islanding during grid disturbances.

Other Application Domains

Our methods have been applied to atomic force microscopy control, chemical process control, reverse flow reactors, communication systems, epidemiological modeling, and various other domains requiring analysis and control of complex nonlinear dynamics.
