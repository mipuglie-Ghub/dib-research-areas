Analysis and Control of Piecewise-smooth and Hybrid Systems

Many dynamical systems exhibit discontinuous behavior due to impacts, switching, friction, or designed discontinuous control laws. Our group has pioneered the mathematical analysis of such piecewise-smooth (PWS) and hybrid systems, developing comprehensive theories for their bifurcations, stability, and control.

We have established fundamental results on discontinuity-induced bifurcations, including grazing, sliding, and border-collision phenomena. Our contraction theory approach for switched systems provides powerful tools for analyzing convergence and synchronization in networks of PWS systems. This work has been recognized through our book on piecewise-smooth dynamical systems and numerous contributions to the field over more than two decades.

Core research topics

  • Discontinuity-induced bifurcations (grazing, sliding, border-collision)
  • Contraction and incremental stability analysis for switched systems
  • Synchronization in networks of piecewise-smooth systems
  • Hybrid model reference adaptive control
  • Switched control design and hybrid systems
  • State observer design for discontinuous systems
  • Filippov systems and differential inclusions
  • Normal form theory for nonsmooth bifurcations
  • Corner-impact and sliding bifurcations
  • Bifurcation control and tailoring

Related applications

Power converters, mechanical systems with friction and impact, automotive control, relay feedback systems, cam-follower systems, dry-friction oscillators
