Learning-Enhanced Control and Data-Driven Methods

Modern control systems increasingly operate in environments that are too complex to model accurately from first principles. Our research develops methods that combine classical control-theoretic guarantees with data-driven learning to achieve robust performance in uncertain, high-dimensional systems.

Building on our foundational work in model reference adaptive control (MRAC) and minimal control synthesis (MCS), we now focus on reinforcement learning and neural network-based approaches that respect the structure of complex dynamical systems. Recent developments in control-tutored reinforcement learning demonstrate how to embed control-theoretic knowledge into learning algorithms, achieving better sample efficiency and safety guarantees while maintaining the flexibility of data-driven methods.

Core research topics

  • Control-tutored deep reinforcement learning
  • Model reference adaptive control (MRAC and MCS algorithms)
  • Safe learning with control barrier functions
  • Data-driven model identification and system reconstruction
  • Reward shaping for guaranteed performance
  • Physics-informed neural networks for control

Related applications

Robotic control, human-avatar interaction, motor rehabilitation, motor coordination, phase estimation in biological rhythms, autonomous navigation
